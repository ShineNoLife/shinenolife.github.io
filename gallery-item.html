Description

Our quantum computing laboratory represents a cutting-edge facility dedicated to advancing the frontiers of quantum information science. Established in 2022 with funding from the National Science Foundation, this lab houses one of the few IBM Quantum System One installations at an academic institution.

The lab features a fully isolated environment with precise temperature and humidity controls to maintain the stability of our quantum systems. Our cryogenic infrastructure maintains qubits at temperatures near absolute zero, while our custom control electronics enable precise manipulation of quantum states.

Key research conducted in this facility includes:

  • Development of error-corrected quantum algorithms
  • Quantum machine learning applications
  • Quantum simulation of complex molecular systems
  • Hybrid quantum-classical computing approaches

The lab serves as a collaborative hub, bringing together researchers from computer science, physics, chemistry, and engineering to tackle interdisciplinary challenges in quantum computing.
